NAVULTRA Registry: Navitor vs SAPIEN 3 Ultra in Small Annuli

Authors: Cannata S, Sultan I, Van Mieghem N, Latib A, et al. (International Multicenter)
Journal: EuroIntervention | February 2026 | DOI: 10.4244/EIJ-D-25-00937 | üîó Full Text

Executive Summary

Real-world multicenter registry comparing the intra-annular self-expanding Navitor valve vs balloon-expandable SAPIEN 3 Ultra in patients with small aortic annuli. Head-to-head comparison in the most challenging TAVR subset.

Key Points:

  • Head-to-head comparison of two leading intra-annular valve designs
  • Real-world data from high-volume international centers
  • Focuses on small annulus patients‚Äîhistorically difficult population
  • Compares hemodynamic performance, PPM rates, and clinical outcomes
  • Guides device selection based on anatomical considerations
This registry provides the comparative data we've been waiting for. When you have a small annulus case, this helps guide the Navitor vs S3 Ultra decision. Pay attention to the PPM and gradient differences.

Executive Summary

First 5-year durability data for the early-generation Intrepid transapical TMVR system. Critical long-term follow-up as TMVR technology matures and transfemoral approaches emerge.

Key Points:

  • 5-year follow-up‚Äîlongest TMVR durability data available

COMPARE Trial: 5-Year Results of Low vs High-Dose DCB for Femoropopliteal Disease

Authors: Wittig T, Schmidt A, Zeller T, Tepe G, Scheinert D, Steiner S, et al.
Journal: EuroIntervention | February 2026 | DOI: 10.4244/EIJ-D-25-00904 | üîó Full Text

Executive Summary

5-year outcomes from the prospective randomized COMPARE trial comparing low-dose vs high-dose paclitaxel drug-coated balloons for femoropopliteal lesions. Important long-term safety and efficacy data for DCB therapy.

Key Findings:

  • Randomized comparison of two DCB dosing strategies
  • 5-year durability and patency outcomes
  • Addresses long-term safety concerns around paclitaxel
  • Guides DCB selection in peripheral intervention
While peripheral isn't your primary focus, this data is relevant when consulting on CAD patients with PAD. The paclitaxel dose question has implications for both coronary and peripheral DCB use.

PCI Outcomes After TAVR: Impact of Valve Frame Height

Authors: Pivato CA, Di Angelantonio E, Stefanini G, et al. (International Multicenter)
Journal: JACC Cardiovasc Interv | October 2025 | üîó PubMed

Executive Summary

Large multicenter registry examining PCI outcomes in post-TAVR patients, stratified by valve frame height. Addresses the critical question: does valve choice at TAVR impact future coronary interventions?

Key Findings:

  • Tall-framed valves (TFVs) associated with more challenging coronary access
  • Higher rates of procedural complexity with certain valve designs
  • Important implications for initial valve selection, especially in patients with CAD
  • Provides data to guide Heart Team discussions on valve choice
When selecting a TAVR valve in patients with known CAD or high likelihood of future PCI, valve frame height matters. This should factor into your Heart Team discussions‚Äîespecially in younger patients.

Registry Data

TAVR + PCI vs SAVR + CABG: Long-Term Multicenter Registry

Authors: Stundl A, Bleiziffer S, Kupatt C, et al. (German Centers)
Journal: Clin Res Cardiol | September 2025 | üîó PubMed

Executive Summary

Multicenter German registry comparing long-term outcomes in patients with AS + CAD treated with either TAVR+PCI or SAVR+CABG. Critical data for the intermediate-risk population eligible for either approach.

Key Points:

  • Addresses real-world outcomes in dual-pathology patients (AS + CAD)
  • Long-term follow-up data comparing interventional vs surgical approach
  • Intermediate-risk patients are the key population of interest
  • Relevant for Heart Team decision-making in complex cases
This data is directly relevant to your practice. When you have a patient with both AS and significant CAD, the choice between TAVR+PCI vs SAVR+CABG isn't always clear-cut. Registry data like this helps inform those discussions.

Network Meta-Analysis

Optimal Timing for PCI in TAVR Patients: Network Meta-Analysis

Authors: Carvalho PEP, Nascimento BR, Taramasso M, Garot P, et al.
Journal: Cardiovasc Revasc Med | March 2025 | üîó PubMed

Executive Summary

Network meta-analysis comparing outcomes of concomitant PCI+TAVR vs staged PCI before TAVR vs staged PCI after TAVR. Aims to answer: what's the optimal revascularization timing strategy?

Key Findings:

  • Compares three strategies: concomitant, staged before, staged after
  • Analyzes safety and efficacy endpoints
  • Provides hierarchy of approaches based on available evidence
  • Important for procedural planning in CAD + AS patients
The timing question comes up constantly. This meta-analysis provides a framework for discussing revascularization strategy with patients. Consider patient factors: symptom driver, lesion complexity, and procedural risk.

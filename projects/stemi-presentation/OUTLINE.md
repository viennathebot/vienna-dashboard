# STEMI Presentation for EMS
## North Vista Hospital | March 2026

**Presenter:** Dr. Peter Bleszynski, MD, FACC, FSCAI
**Director of Cardiology, North Vista Hospital**

**Duration:** 45-60 minutes
**Audience:** Emergency Medical Services personnel

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this presentation, participants will be able to:
1. Recognize classic and atypical STEMI patterns on 12-lead EKG
2. Identify common STEMI mimics and avoid false cath lab activations
3. Apply current AHA/ACC guideline-directed prehospital management
4. Understand the importance of minimizing first medical contact-to-device time
5. Describe key medications and their roles in ACS management

---

## Presentation Outline

### I. Introduction (5 min)
- Why STEMI matters: Time = Muscle
- Door-to-balloon vs First Medical Contact-to-Device
- The EMS role in the STEMI chain of survival
- North Vista Hospital STEMI activation process

### II. STEMI Pathophysiology (5 min)
- Coronary anatomy basics
- Plaque rupture and thrombosis
- Ischemia → Injury → Infarction timeline
- Salvageable myocardium concept

### III. EKG Recognition - Classic STEMI Patterns (15 min)
- ST-elevation criteria (≥1mm in limb leads, ≥2mm in precordial)
- **Anterior STEMI** (LAD) - V1-V4
- **Inferior STEMI** (RCA/LCx) - II, III, aVF
- **Lateral STEMI** (LCx) - I, aVL, V5-V6
- **Posterior STEMI** - ST depression V1-V3, tall R waves
- **Right Ventricular Infarction** - V4R elevation with inferior STEMI
- Reciprocal changes and their significance
- High-quality EKG examples for each

### IV. STEMI Mimics - Avoiding False Activations (10 min)
- **Left Ventricular Hypertrophy (LVH)** - Strain pattern
- **Left Bundle Branch Block (LBBB)** - Sgarbossa criteria
- **Early Repolarization** - "Smiley face" morphology
- **Pericarditis** - Diffuse ST elevation, PR depression
- **Brugada Pattern** - Right precordial coved ST elevation
- **Hyperkalemia** - Peaked T waves progressing to sine wave
- **Takotsubo Cardiomyopathy** - Anterior STE without culprit lesion
- **Aortic Dissection** - The critical rule-out
- Key differentiating features for each

### V. Prehospital Management - 2023 AHA/ACC Guidelines (10 min)
- **Immediate actions:**
  - 12-lead EKG within 10 minutes of first medical contact
  - Prehospital cath lab activation
  - Aspirin 324mg (chewed, non-enteric)
  - Supplemental O2 only if SpO2 <90%
  - IV access, cardiac monitoring
  
- **Pain management:**
  - Nitroglycerin (with BP/RV infarct precautions)
  - Morphine (cautious use - may increase mortality)
  
- **Anticoagulation:**
  - Unfractionated heparin vs. enoxaparin
  - Timing considerations
  
- **What NOT to do:**
  - Routine oxygen in normoxic patients
  - P2Y12 inhibitors in the field (physician direction only)
  - Delay transport for IV access

### VI. In-Hospital Care Overview (5 min)
- Primary PCI - Gold standard (goal: FMC-to-device <90 min)
- Fibrinolytic therapy - When PCI not available within 120 min
- Rescue PCI after failed fibrinolysis
- CABG indications
- Cardiogenic shock management (Impella, IABP, ECMO)

### VII. Key Medications (5 min)
- **Antiplatelets:** Aspirin, P2Y12 inhibitors (clopidogrel, ticagrelor, prasugrel)
- **Anticoagulants:** Heparin, enoxaparin, bivalirudin
- **Beta-blockers:** Timing and contraindications
- **ACE inhibitors/ARBs:** Post-MI remodeling prevention
- **Statins:** High-intensity therapy
- **Nitrates:** Symptom relief, preload reduction

### VIII. Special Scenarios (5 min)
- Cardiac arrest with STEMI
- Cardiogenic shock
- Elderly/frail patients
- Transfer protocols (non-PCI to PCI center)
- Cocaine-associated MI

### IX. Case Studies (5 min)
- Case 1: Classic anterior STEMI
- Case 2: Inferior STEMI with RV involvement
- Case 3: STEMI mimic - pericarditis
- Interactive Q&A

### X. Summary & Take-Home Points (2 min)
- Recognize STEMI early
- Activate early
- Minimize delays
- Know your mimics
- North Vista contact info and protocols

üìê Fit Tips & Care Guide

For 6'1", 190 lbs, athletic build in Las Vegas heat

Fit Guidelines

üëï Shirts

Go slim fit or "athletic fit" ‚Äî standard fit will tent at the waist. Slim fit shows the build without being painted on. Bonobos and Charles Tyrwhitt both offer athletic cuts with broader shoulders and tapered waist.

üëñ Pants

Slim-straight or tailored fit. Avoid skinny (too tight on quads) and relaxed (too baggy). 32‚Äì34" inseam depending on rise. Get dress pants hemmed to a clean no-break.

üß• Blazers & Suits

Look for structured shoulders that match your frame. Size 42R likely. Unstructured blazers in particular look great on athletic builds because they drape naturally.

ü©≥ Shorts

7" inseam is the sweet spot for 6'1" ‚Äî shows you don't skip leg day without being aggressive about it.

üëï T-Shirts

Crew neck or henley in a slim/fitted cut. Sleeves should hit mid-bicep, not flap in the wind. If it fits in the shoulders, tailor the rest.

üí° Golden Rule

A $15 tee that fits perfectly beats a $100 tee that doesn't. Fit is everything.

üå°Ô∏è

Las Vegas Heat Survival

üßµ Fabrics

Yes: Linen, cotton, chambray, moisture-wicking blends, Tencel, seersucker
No: Heavy wool, polyester (traps heat), thick denim in July

üé® Colors

Lighter tones reflect heat ‚Äî whites, light blues, stone, sage, soft pink. Save darker colors for evening events or indoor conferences.

üèóÔ∏è Construction

Unstructured blazers (no canvas/padding), unlined pants, open-weave knits. Indoor A/C can be aggressive though ‚Äî a light blazer doubles as protection.

üëü Shoes

Breathable loafers > closed lace-ups when possible. No-show socks always. Save the suede for non-monsoon months (avoid Jul‚ÄìSep).

üßπ

Care Tips

üëî Linen

Embrace wrinkles ‚Äî they're the point. Hang to dry. Light steam, never heavy iron.

üëñ Chinos

Wash cold, hang dry. Ironing is optional ‚Äî a slight rumple is fine for casual.

ü§µ Suits

Dry clean sparingly (2‚Äì3√ó per season). Hang on proper wooden hangers. Steam between cleans.

üëû Dress Shoes

Use cedar shoe trees always. Condition leather monthly. Polish before events. Resole instead of replacing.

üëü White Sneakers

Clean weekly ‚Äî Jason Markk or Magic Eraser. Throw laces in a mesh bag in the wash.

üëï T-Shirts

Wash inside-out in cold water. Air dry to maintain shape. Replace when they stretch out ‚Äî they're $15.

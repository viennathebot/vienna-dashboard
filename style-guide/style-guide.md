## üå°Ô∏è Las Vegas Heat Considerations

Las Vegas spring/summer means **95‚Äì115¬∞F**. Every piece in this guide accounts for that reality:

- **Fabrics**: Linen, cotton, chambray, moisture-wicking blends, Tencel, seersucker
- **Colors**: Lighter tones reflect heat ‚Äî whites, light blues, stone, sage, soft pink
- **Construction**: Unstructured blazers (no canvas/padding), unlined pants, open-weave knits
- **Layering**: Minimal. One layer max over a shirt. Indoor A/C can be aggressive though, so a light blazer doubles as protection
- **Shoes**: Breathable loafers > closed lace-ups when possible. No-show socks always

## üìê Fit Tips for His Body Type

**6'1", 190 lbs, Athletic Build**

- **Shirts**: Slim fit or "athletic fit" ‚Äî standard fit will tent at the waist; slim fit will show the build without being painted on. Brands like Bonobos and Charles Tyrwhitt offer "athletic" cuts with broader shoulders and tapered waist
- **Pants**: Slim-straight or tailored fit. Avoid skinny (too tight on quads) and relaxed (too baggy). 32‚Äì34" inseam depending on the rise
- **Blazers**: Look for structured shoulders that match your frame. Size 42R likely. Unstructured blazers in particular look great on athletic builds because they drape naturally
- **Shorts**: 7" inseam is the sweet spot ‚Äî shows you don't skip leg day without being aggressive about it
- **T-shirts**: Crew neck or henley in a slim/fitted cut. The sleeves should hit mid-bicep, not flap in the wind
- **General rule**: If it fits in the shoulders, tailor the rest. A $15 tee that fits perfectly beats a $100 tee that doesn't

## üìù Care Tips

- **Linen**: Embrace wrinkles ‚Äî they're the point. Hang to dry. Light steam, never heavy iron
- **Chinos**: Wash cold, hang dry. Ironing is optional ‚Äî a slight rumple is fine for casual
- **Suits**: Dry clean sparingly (2‚Äì3x per season). Hang on proper wooden hangers. Steam between cleans
- **Dress shoes**: Use cedar shoe trees always. Condition leather monthly. Polish before events
- **White sneakers**: Clean weekly. Jason Markk or Magic Eraser. Throw laces in a mesh bag in the wash
- **T-shirts**: Wash inside-out in cold water. Air dry to maintain shape. Replace when they stretch out ‚Äî they're $15

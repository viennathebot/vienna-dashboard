### Why Rotate on an AWD Quattro?

- AWD systems distribute power to all four wheels â€” uneven tire wear causes the system to work harder
- Mismatched tire diameters (from uneven wear) can damage the Torsen center differential
- Even wear extends tire life by 20â€“30%
- Your tires are NOT staggered (same 255/40ZR19 all around) â€” rotation is possible and recommended

### Tools Needed

- 4 jack stands (see Section 7)
- 17mm lug bolt socket (the A7 uses lug bolts, not lug nuts)
- Torque wrench (lug bolt torque: **120 Nm / 89 ft-lbs**)

### Step-by-Step Instructions

#### Preparation
1. Park on a **flat, level surface** (garage is ideal).
2. **Mark each tire** with chalk: LF, RF, LR, RR so you know where each started.
3. **Loosen ALL lug bolts** slightly (1/4 turn) while all four tires are still on the ground. This is critical â€” don't try to loosen them in the air.

4. Using the floor jack, **lift the front of the car** first using the front jack point (see Section 7).
5. **Place jack stands** under both front reinforced jack points.
6. **Lower the car onto the front jack stands**.
7. Repeat: **lift the rear** and place jack stands under both rear jack points.
8. **Confirm the car is stable** on all four stands â€” give it a firm push to test.

#### Rotate the Tires
9. **Remove all four wheels** â€” the A7 uses lug bolts that thread INTO the hub (not lug nuts on studs). Be ready to hold the wheel as you remove the last bolt.

> ğŸ’¡ **Pro Tip:** Thread in an alignment pin/guide bolt (or just leave one bolt finger-tight) to hold the wheel while removing/installing the others. You can buy Audi wheel alignment pins cheaply on Amazon.

10. Following the **X-pattern**, move each wheel to its new position:
    - Left Front â†’ Right Rear
    - Right Front â†’ Left Rear  
    - Left Rear â†’ Right Front
    - Right Rear â†’ Left Front

11. **Hand-thread all lug bolts** first to avoid cross-threading. The A7 takes **5 lug bolts per wheel**.
12. **Tighten in a star pattern** â€” don't tighten them in a circle. This ensures even seating.
13. **Lower the car** off the jack stands.
14. **Final torque** all lug bolts to **120 Nm (89 ft-lbs)** in a star pattern with the car on the ground.

#### Post-Rotation
15. **Check tire pressures** â€” set all four to **36 PSI** (cold) per the door jamb sticker for 19" wheels.
16. **Reset the TPMS** if needed through the MMI: CAR â†’ Tire Pressure â†’ Store Tire Pressures.

### Why This Is Harder on the A7 3.0T

The Audi 3.0T supercharged engine has the supercharger and intake manifold sitting **on top** of the engine, directly above the spark plugs. On the front bank (passenger side, cylinders 1-3), you can access the plugs relatively easily by removing the engine cover and some intake components. **The supercharger/intake manifold does NOT need to be fully removed**, but you'll need to remove the engine beauty cover, throttle body area, and some intake components to reach the rear bank.

### Step-by-Step Instructions

#### Front Bank (Cylinders 1-3 â€” Passenger Side) â€” Easier

3. **Remove the coil pack bolts** â€” one **T30 Torx bolt** per coil.
4. **Pull each coil pack straight up** â€” twist slightly and pull. They can be stuck due to heat.
5. **Blow out the spark plug wells** with compressed air â€” you don't want debris falling into the cylinder when you remove the plug.
6. **Insert the spark plug socket** with extension into the well.
7. **Loosen and remove each spark plug** â€” turn counterclockwise. They'll be tight.
8. **Inspect the old plugs** â€” check for oil, carbon, damage. Normal wear shows a light tan electrode.
9. **Apply a tiny dab of anti-seize** to the new plug threads (optional â€” some mechanics skip this on NGK iridiums).
10. **Install the new plug by hand first** â€” thread it in by hand (or using a piece of rubber hose over the plug to turn it) to avoid cross-threading. THIS IS CRITICAL.
11. **Torque to 25 Nm (18 ft-lbs)** â€” do NOT overtighten. Aluminum heads strip easily.
12. **Apply a thin coat of dielectric grease** inside the coil pack boot.
13. **Reinstall coil packs** â€” push firmly until they click/seat.
14. **Reinstall coil pack bolts** and reconnect the electrical connectors.

15. **Remove additional intake components** to access the rear bank. This typically involves:

21. **Reinstall all rear coil packs, bolts, and connectors**.

24. **Start the engine** â€” it should start smoothly with no misfires.
25. **Check for any codes** â€” if you have an OBD2 scanner, verify no fault codes.
26. **Test drive** â€” accelerate smoothly through the RPM range. Any hesitation or misfire means a plug may not be seated or a coil isn't connected.

5. **Release the wire from any routing clips** along the suspension/brake line â€” note where each clip is for reinstallation.

7. **Install the new sensor** â€” push the probe firmly into the brake pad hole until it clicks/seats.
8. **Route the new wire** following the same path as the old one, securing it in all the same clips.
9. **Connect the electrical plug** â€” push until it clicks.
10. **Reinstall the wheel** and torque lug bolts to **120 Nm (89 ft-lbs)**.

#### Rear Brake Wear Sensor (Rear Right Wheel)

11. Repeat the same process on the **rear right wheel**.
12. The rear sensor wire is typically shorter and easier to access.

#### Reset the Warning
13. The brake pad warning on the dash should clear automatically after the new sensor is connected and you drive for a few minutes.
14. If it doesn't clear, use an OBD2 scanner (VCDS/OBDeleven) to clear the fault code.
